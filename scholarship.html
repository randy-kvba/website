"Boogie Woogie" Bob Peters Scholarship

An annual $1,500 award honoring the legacy of a beloved Kalamazoo blues legend — and investing in the next generation of musicians.

In His Memory

The Legacy of "Boogie Woogie" Bob Peters

Bob Peters was a fixture on the Kalamazoo music landscape for decades — a gifted pianist, guitar player, and music historian who was beloved throughout the Southwest Michigan blues community.

A multi-talented artist and tireless champion of the blues, Peters left behind a legacy of music, art, and deep relationships that his family, friends, and loyal fans strive to keep alive. He passed away suddenly on March 13, 2010.

In 2013, the KVBA and the Peters Family joined forces to create a scholarship fund dedicated to his memory and musical legacy. A vision of keeping that passion alive and passing it along to future generations became the mission of the fund.

"Keeping the legacy and passion alive and passing it along for future generations."

— The Peters Family and KVBA Board of Directors

$1,500 Annual Scholarship Award

Sponsored by the Bob Peters Family and the Kalamazoo Valley Blues Association. Awarded each year to a graduating senior from a Southwest Michigan school who demonstrates excellence in music and academics.

Who Can Apply

Eligibility & Requirements

This scholarship is open to graduating seniors who have been active in music programs throughout their high school career.

  • 1
    Active in Music Must have been active in a music program — vocal or instrumental — during their high school career, and in the community.
  • 2
    College / University Acceptance Must have been accepted to a college or university. Proof of program acceptance in the form of an official signature will be required.
  • 3
    Essay or Video Submission Write a 2-page, double-spaced essay or submit a 3–5 minute video on the topic: "What Contributions has Blues Music Made to Our American Culture?" Be sure to footnote your sources.
  • 4
    Music Involvement & Depth In addition to the essay or video, emphasis will be placed on the number of years a student has been in music classes and extra musical endeavors — musicals, special ensembles, music outside of school, etc.
  • 5
    Southwest Michigan School Qualified candidates must be students of a Southwest Michigan high school.
